## How did my body feel?
Legs surprisingly good. Right knee a little stiff in the morning but loosened up after the first climb. Lower back felt the saddle by kilometre 70.

## Best moment of the day
Descending into Genova as the late afternoon sun turned the sea completely flat and golden. Stood on the pedals for a full minute just staring.

## Person I met
Marco, a retired fisherman on the harbour wall who insisted I try his wife's focaccia di Recco. We talked for forty minutes about the Ligurian coast, his sons in Milan, and why nobody cycles here anymore.

## What did I eat?
Focaccia di Recco with fresh cheese, a plate of trofie al pesto, two litres of mineral water, and an espresso that arrived in a cup the size of a thimble.

## Landscape & nature
Rocky Ligurian coast all day — dramatic grey cliffs falling straight into turquoise water, pine forests clinging to impossible slopes, tiny villages painted in faded orange and yellow stacked above the sea.

## Smells & details
Salt air and diesel at the port in the morning. Wild rosemary and thyme on the climbs. Someone grilling fish somewhere above Camogli around midday.

## How do I feel overall?
Excited. Slightly overwhelmed by the scale of what I've started. Exactly where I should be.
