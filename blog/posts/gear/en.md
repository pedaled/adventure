Packing for 40 days on a bike through Italy is a different problem than packing for a weekend. You need enough to be self-sufficient, light enough to climb, and simple enough to fix anything that breaks on the side of the road in a village where nobody speaks English.

[photo:1]

After three test weekends and a lot of spreadsheet time, here's what makes the cut.

## The bags

Four bags total: a frame bag, a handlebar roll, a saddle bag, and a small top tube bag for things I need without stopping. Everything else stays home.

## Clothing

Seven days of cycling kit that rotates. Two pairs of bibs, three jerseys, one pair of arm warmers, one light rain jacket. In Italy in May the rain is brief and the sun is reliable — no need for full winter kit.

## Tools & spares

Two spare tubes, a patch kit, tyre levers, a multitool, a chain tool, a spare chain link, and enough cable to fix both brakes and both derailleurs. If something bigger breaks, I find a bike shop. Italy has bike shops.

[photo:2]

## Electronics

Phone, battery bank, GPS computer, lights front and rear, earphones, one small plug adaptor. Everything charges from USB. The battery bank covers two full days without a socket.

## The one rule

If it doesn't earn its weight every single day, it doesn't come. The focaccia does not count against this rule.
